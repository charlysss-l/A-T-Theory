Self-Efficacy Theory of Motivation

known as Social Cognitive Theory or Social Learning Theory by Albert Bandura

What is Self-Efficacy?

Self-efficacy is a key concept in motivation and learning. It is the belief in one's own abilities to succeed in specific tasks or situations.

Historical Background

Albert Bandura's work was deeply influenced by earlier behaviorist theories.

Early Foundations

Bandura's work was influenced by previous behaviorist theories, notably those of B.F. Skinner and John Watson, who emphasized external reinforcement as a primary driver of behavior. However, Bandurarecognized that these theories did not adequately account for the role of internal cognitive processes, like thinking, memory, and attention, involved in learning

The Bobo Doll Experiment

1961

Bandura’s landmark study, the Bobo doll experiment, was central in the development of Social Learning Theory. In this experiment, children who observed an adult model behaving aggressively toward a Bobo doll were more likely to later imitate the aggressive behavior themselves. This demonstrated that people could learn behaviors through observation without direct reinforcement. The findings suggested that learning is not just a matter of responding to stimuli but also involves cognitive processes such as attention, retention, reproduction, and motivation.

The Shift to Social Cognitive Theory

1980s

In the 1980s, Bandura expanded his ideas into Social Cognitive Theory, which emphasized the interaction between personal, behavioral, and environmental factors in shaping behavior. A key concept in SCT is reciprocal determinism, where these three factors (person, behavior, and environment) constantly influence each other.

Key Concepts

  • Observational Learning:People can learn by watching others (models), which is also known as modeling.

  • Reciprocal Determinism:The idea that personal factors, behavior, and environmental influences all operate together to influence learning and behavior.

  • Self-Efficacy: A person’s belief in their ability to succeed in specific situations, which influences their actions and persistence.

Albert Bandura's Legacy

Albert Bandura’s work on self-efficacy and social cognitive theory has had a profound impact on fields ranging from psychology to education. His theory has guided research on motivation, learning, and behavior, and continues to influence how we understand human development and achievement.
